The boy who cried wolf picture

There lived a boy in the village. The boy had the responsibility of watching over the flock. He was easily bored and craved attention. The young boy falsely cries “wolf” for fun, causing villagers to come to his aid. But, when they arrived, they found no wolf. He repeated this several times, fooling the villagers. The villagers got frustrated with coming to help and found the boy making fun of them. One day, when a real wolf eventually appears and threatens the flock of sheep, the boy cries for help again, but this time no one believes him. The villagers recalled the trick he played. The only time he really needed the villagemen, nobody appeared. The village men thought that the young boy was making fun by yelling for help again. The wolf attacked the flock of sheep, and several were lost. The moral of the story is that lying or exaggerating the truth can lead to a loss of trust


Old Parents

There lived a family consisting of the old parents, his son, his son’s wife, and children. A man was upset with his old parents. Sometimes, he becomes so angry that he even beats them. One day, he threw them out of his house in anger. Both of the parents left the house sadly and never turned around. Now, the man lived happily with his wife and children. Twenty years later, his children had grown up, and all of them had gotten married. They are doing the same thing with men as he used to do with his old parents. He remembered those dates and was deeply saddened by the atrocities that he had committed against his parents.


The Tortoise and The Rabbit Picture.

Once upon a time, in a peaceful meadow, there lived a boastful and speedy hare who loved to show off his speed and a slow, patient tortoise. Once the tortoise decided to challenge the hare to a race, The hare agreed to race, thinking it would be an easy victory. The race began with a signal, and the hare dashed ahead, leaving the tortoise far behind. He was so confident in his speed that he decided to take a nap under a tree, thinking he had plenty of time to win the race. The tortoise, on the other hand, continued to plod along slowly and steadily, never stopping. The hare woke up after a while, realized his mistake, and rushed to catch up with the tortoise. But it was too late. The tortoise crossed the finish line first while the hare was asleep. The tortoise wins the race. The other animals cheered and celebrated the tortoise’s victory. The hare was ashamed and embarrassed by his overconfidence and laziness.


The two friends

There was a village where two friends lived. The friends had a close bond with one another. One of the friends was rich, and the other was poor. The wealthy friend was generous and always willing to assist the other friend. He usually asked him for money whenever he needed it so that he could assist him. The poor friend was never given the opportunity to request the money. One day, the poor friend found himself in desperate need of money and decided to seek the assistance of his wealthy friend. When he asked his wealthy friend for assistance, he declined with an excuse.
